HEALTH CONCERNS

Different chemicals can leach from plastics to human bodies resulting in endocrine disruption, weight gain, insulin resistance, decreased reproductive health and cancer.
Plastics often contain harmful chemicals like bisphenol A (BPA),phthalates,and flame retardents. They breakdown and form microplastics like tiny particles that can be ingested and inhaled which can accumulate in the body and may cause inflammation

FLORA AND FAUNA

The cumulative effect of plastic pollution can lead to the decline of species population, loss of biodiversity,and discription of ecosystem services.It can degrade slowly and release toxic chemicals into the soil,affecting soil quality. Wildlife can become entangled in plastic waste such as fishing nets,six-pack rings,and other debris. it's waste can alter and damage natural habitats such as coral reefs and beaches.

Production Emissions

Plastics are primarily made from fossil fuels (petroleum and natural gas). The extraction and refining of these materials release significant amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane, both potent greenhouse gases.The energy required for plastic production often comes from burning fossil fuels, further contributing to GHG emission
